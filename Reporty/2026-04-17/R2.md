## [3] PILLAR ANALYSIS

### PILLAR 1 — LIQUIDITY & FED POLICY | Score: 3.0 / 10

Liquidity first — always. Where does it stand?

RRP 137M. This number alone says little — but in the context of 2.4T at the peak (2022) it says everything. The system has consumed nearly all excess liquidity. It is not a crisis state, but it is not room for an impulse. Every dollar leaving RRP and entering the system is a dollar that was previously stored there as excess reserve. Those reserves are almost gone.

M2 YoY 4.88% — at first glance healthy. But this is a nominal number. Real M2 (adjusted for inflation ~3%) is close to zero. Real money supply is not growing. Liquidity support for the market is therefore neutral, not expansionary.

Yield curve: 10Y 4.25%, 2Y 3.78%, spread 0.55. Both positive after normalizing from inversion. Critical question: what type of steepener? Looking at the context — rising term premium, tariffs, fiscal deficit, sovereign downgrade — this is a BEAR STEEPENER. 10Y is being pulled up by risk premium, not by a Fed pivot. And a bear steepener is historically one of the worst regimes for equities — it increases the discount rate, compresses valuations.

Every +1% on 10Y ≈ −15 to −20% fair value SPX. If 10Y rises to 4.75% (a real threat with further deficit financing), the math is brutal: implied fair value compression of −7 to −9% from this source alone.

Real FFR 1.28% — restrictive, but not aggressively so. The Fed is not in panic. But it has no impulse to cut. Core PCE 2.97% keeps its hands tied. This means: no Fed put in sight.

### PILLAR 2 — YIELD CURVE & CREDIT ENVIRONMENT | Score: 4.5 / 10

HY spreads 286bp — risk-on band (<300bp). IG 81bp on the edge of optimism. On the surface: a healthy credit market. But here is a paradox that concerns me more than anything else.

HY 286bp says: the market sees no credit risk. ERP −0.98% says: the market does not want to be compensated for equity risk. These two things cannot both be true indefinitely. Either credit spreads are too compressed and will soon expand — or equities are too expensive and will soon correct. In either case, the equity investor is on the wrong side of the trade.

Cooperative signal from R5 (TA): XLF rel str −2.67% over the past 20 days. Banks are lagging SPY. Banks are a leading indicator of credit conditions — if the financial sector underperforms, it means that the market is beginning to reprice credit conditions negatively ahead of HY spreads. This is an early warning signal, not a confirmation.

SLOOS 5.3% — mild tightening. Unfavorable, but far from an alarm. CC Delinquency 2.94% — exceeds the watch threshold of 2.5%. The consumer is starting to struggle. The cascade mechanism: CC delinquency → bankruptcies → HY spread widening → forced selling. We are in the first act.
