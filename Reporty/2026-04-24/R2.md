### 7C — Identified blind spots

Three blind spots surfaced this week that I want to document.

First, my framework weighted M2 YoY heavily as a liquidity indicator, but M2 is a stock variable and stock variables hide flow deterioration. RRP is a flow-like indicator (it reflects the change in excess bank liquidity week-over-week) and it caught what M2 missed. Going forward I'm weighting RRP delta more heavily and M2 YoY level less heavily — at least while RRP is near-empty and volatile.

Second, I did not have oil in my primary trigger list last week, and WTI's move from $84 to $95 is materially affecting three of six pillars (Pillar 1 via 10Y, Pillar 5 via consumer stress, Pillar 6 directly). Adding WTI > $90 sustained for 2+ weeks as an explicit trigger going forward.

Third, my H2 hypothesis (XLF predicts HY widening) was strongly validated this week — XLF moved in the predicted direction while HY stayed flat. But my prompt didn't have XLF as a named sub-metric inside Pillar 2; it was treated as a cooperation signal from R5. This week I'm promoting XLF to a named sub-metric with explicit weight inside Pillar 2. The lead-indicator nature of financial-sector relative strength has proven itself enough to deserve structural weight, not just narrative reference.
