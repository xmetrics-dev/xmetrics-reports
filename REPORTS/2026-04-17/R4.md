### PILLAR 2 — VALUATION FRAGILITY & PAYOFF CONVEXITY | Score: 1.5 / 10

Negative ERP = concave payoff. That is not a metaphor — it is a mathematical definition.

Convex payoff: small investments, large potential return. Equities with ERP +5%: for every $100 of risk you receive a $5 premium. Concave payoff: large investments, small potential return, large downside exposure. Equities with ERP −0.98%: for every $100 of risk you pay a $0.98 premium on top of the risk-free bond. Typical of central-bank-distorted environments just before a correction.

CAPE_PREMIUM = (40.44 / 17) − 1 = **+138%** above historical median
MEAN_REVERSION = 1 − (17 / 40.44) = **−58.0%** decline to normalization

Lindy effect: CAPE has been above 35 since approximately 2019 (with a brief dip in 2020). That is 5+ years of extreme mispricing. Lindy says: the longer the excess lasts, the greater the correction will be. The system does not revert to normal — it reverts below normal. Mean reversion in history was not to 17x — it was to 10–12x, then back up.

HY 286bp with negative ERP = double fragility. The credit market is accepting risk for 2.86% above T-bills. The equity market accepts no compensation. This divergence is unstable.
